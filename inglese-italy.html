Italy: Venice and Florence

Managing overtourism in Italy's most fragile historic cities.

CitiesVenice, Florence
Tools usedContributo di Accesso (Venice); street regulation (Florence)
Since2024 – 2025

Venice introduced the Contributo di Accesso, an entry fee for day-trippers on high-traffic dates, in 2024. Florence took a regulation-based approach instead, restricting outdoor dining and advertising across 73 historic streets to protect the character of its historic centre. National measures in 2025 added tourist taxes and restrictions on short-term rentals in Florence, Rome and Venice, directly addressing SDG 10 by protecting residents' access to affordable housing.

A critical look at the evidence: visitor numbers on Venice's most crowded days fell only slightly after the fee was introduced - a useful reminder that pricing tools are a partial, not a complete, solution to overtourism, and work better for gathering data than for genuinely changing tourist behaviour.

In sintesi

  • Venice introduced an entry fee for day-trippers (Contributo di Accesso) in 2024, with mixed results on actual visitor numbers.
  • Florence took a regulation-based approach, restricting outdoor dining and advertising across 73 historic streets.
  • 2025 national measures added tourist taxes and restrictions on short-term rentals in Florence, Rome and Venice.
  • Pricing tools work better for gathering data and revenue than for genuinely changing tourist behaviour.
